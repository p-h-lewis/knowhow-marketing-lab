## Session 1 — Google Ads Grants, Local SEO & AI Tools

**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Main topics covered:**

### Topic 1: Google Ads Grants for Nonprofits & Religious Organisations
- Google Ad Grants give nonprofits $10,000/month in free ad spend
- Religious organisations in the US qualify as nonprofits
- Strategy: sell them a website first (required for grant approval), then help apply for the grant, then manage the ads
- Key requirement: the website must have a donation/payment processor backend
- Tip: you don't need to build the website — just manage the ads and tell them over time that their site doesn't convert

### Topic 2: Google Ads — Client Account Setup & Access
- How to have a client sign up for their own Google Ads account (not under your MCC)
- Google Ads referral credits: clients signing up via a referral link earn ad credits to jumpstart their first campaign
- How to add your agency as a manager to a client's account
- Why you need a thank-you page that can't be seen publicly but tracks form completions for conversion tracking

### Topic 3: AI Tools — Manus for Building Funnels & Websites
- Live demo: Pip showed a Stripe-integrated funnel built in Manus
- The funnel connects to GHL: client buys → fills out onboarding form → goes into GHL automatically
- GoHighLevel has added a Manus integration
- Using Manus to build client websites for $100–$200 as a low-cost entry offer

### Topic 4: AI Tools — ChatGPT vs Gemini for Marketing
- Gemini in Google Workspace: available as a paid add-on (~$20/month)
- Gemini can read your Gmail, Docs, and Sheets — useful for scheduling reports
- ChatGPT advantage: organised conversation history that people don't want to lose
- Scheduling Google Ads reports → forwarding to Gemini in Workspace for automated analysis
- Legacy pricing on tools (Descript, Gemini) — don't change plans if you're grandfathered in

### Topic 5: Local SEO — Schema & Screaming Frog
- Schema markup: add schema to every page, not just the homepage
- Local businesses: use LocalBusiness schema, not OrganisationSchema
- Blog posts: use BlogPosting schema
- Screaming Frog with AI integration: can now suggest schema for each page
- How to implement schema: custom HTML at the bottom of the page (or via a WordPress plugin like Rank Math)

**Key takeaways for members:**
1. Google Ads Grants are an underused revenue stream — every nonprofit client is an opportunity
2. Always set up a thank-you page for conversion tracking before running any ads
3. Manus can build client funnels with Stripe + GHL integration — this is a legitimate low-cost service offering
4. Schema on every page is now table stakes for SEO and AI search visibility
5. If you're paying for Google Workspace, you're probably not using Gemini — it's worth exploring

**Homework:** Audit one of your client websites using Screaming Frog. Check which pages are missing schema. Add LocalBusiness or OrganisationSchema to the homepage if it's not there.

## Session 2 — Schema Deep Dive, AI SEO & Content Optimisation

**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Main topics covered:**

### Topic 1: Crawled But Not Indexed — What To Do
- Pages that are crawled but not indexed: a common GSC issue
- How to request indexing in Google Search Console
- When NOT to request indexing: thin content pages, duplicate pages, pages with no backlinks
- Strategy: fix the page first (add content, fix schema, improve speed), then request indexing

### Topic 2: Schema Markup — A Practical Deep Dive
- Schema is now critical for both traditional SEO and AI search (GEO)
- FAQ schema: add to any page that has questions and answers
- Review schema: only valid on pages that actually collect reviews (not just testimonials)
- BlogPosting schema: required on every blog post for AI citation eligibility
- How to check your schema: use the Schema.org validator or Google's Rich Results Test
- Screaming Frog + AI: can audit your entire site and suggest schema type per page
- Implementation: custom HTML at the bottom of the page (WordPress: Rank Math plugin handles this)

### Topic 3: AI SEO — Writing Content for AI Overviews & LLMs
- AI Overviews (Google) and LLMs (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) pull from structured, factual content
- Key principle: write at a Grade 8 reading level — clear, direct, factual sentences
- What AI tools look for: question → direct answer → supporting evidence
- Avoid verbose, long-winded AI-generated copy — it signals low quality to both humans and AI
- Optimising social posts for AI: use keywords naturally, write factual statements, avoid fluff
- How to check if you're appearing in AI overviews: search your target keywords in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google

### Topic 4: Content Strategy — AI Tools for Refinement
- Using ChatGPT to refine content: ask for 3 options on how to improve a post, then choose the angle
- Don't ask AI to write from scratch — use it to improve your own ideas
- Gemini in Workspace: can read your existing content and suggest improvements
- Social posting: optimise captions with Grade 8 language and factual statements

### Topic 5: Screaming Frog — Practical Walkthrough
- How to use Screaming Frog to audit a site for schema gaps
- Paid vs free version: paid version has AI integration and no crawl limit
- What to look for: missing meta descriptions, duplicate titles, broken links, missing schema
- How to export the report and use it with ChatGPT to prioritise fixes

**Key takeaways for members:**
1. Schema is no longer optional — it's required for AI search visibility
2. Write content for humans first, AI second: clear, direct, factual, Grade 8 level
3. Screaming Frog is the fastest way to find schema gaps across an entire site
4. Crawled but not indexed = Google doesn't trust the page yet — fix the content before requesting indexing
5. AI tools work best when you give them your own ideas to refine, not when you ask them to create from scratch

**Homework:** Run your website through the Google Rich Results Test (search.google.com/test/rich-results). Check which pages have valid schema and which don't. Fix the homepage first.

## Session 3 — Keyword Research, Topic Clusters, Search Console & AI SEO

**Duration:** ~62 minutes
**Main topics covered:**

### Topic 1: Google Ads — Client Account Setup & Referral Credits
- How to set up a client's own Google Ads account (not under your MCC)
- Google Ads referral program: clients earn ad credits when they sign up via a referral link
- Why this matters: the client owns the account, you manage it — cleaner for everyone
- Conversion tracking: always set up a thank-you page before running any ads
- Connecting Google Analytics audiences to Google Ads for remarketing

### Topic 2: Keyword Research Has Changed — Think Topic Clusters
- Traditional keyword research (one keyword per page) is outdated
- Modern SEO: think in topic clusters — one pillar page + multiple supporting pages
- How to use a keyword strategy builder (e.g., Semrush, Ahrefs) to find cluster opportunities
- Don't try to rank for every suggested keyword — pick the ones that match your audience's intent
- AI SEO angle: topic clusters help AI models understand your site's authority on a subject
- Claude and OpenAI were caught by Google scraping content — this is why original, structured content matters more than ever

### Topic 3: Search Console — Practical Walkthrough
- How to add a domain to Google Search Console (domain property vs URL prefix)
- Use Cloudflare for domain verification (fastest method)
- How to find what your site is already ranking for: Performance → Queries
- Using GSC data to find content optimisation opportunities: pages ranking on page 2 (positions 11–20) are the best quick wins
- How to check if AI is citing your content: search your brand name in ChatGPT and Perplexity

### Topic 4: AI SEO — Optimising for AI Overviews
- AI Overviews pull from pages with clear question-and-answer structure
- Structure your content: H2 = question, paragraph = direct answer, then supporting detail
- Factual, specific content outranks vague, general content in AI results
- How to check: search your target topic in Google with AI Overviews enabled — see who's cited
- Tip: if a competitor is cited, look at how their page is structured and replicate the pattern

### Topic 5: Shopify SEO — Technical Considerations
- Shopify uses Ruby — some SEO plugins behave differently than WordPress
- Cloudflare: put your domain through Cloudflare for speed and security (free tier is sufficient)
- Chrome extensions can interfere with SEO audits — turn them all off before auditing a client site
- Cache issues: clear cache and cookies before testing page speed or schema
- Local businesses: always add the domain as a separate property in Search Console (not just the subdomain)

**Key takeaways for members:**
1. Stop thinking in single keywords — build topic clusters around your core subjects
2. Search Console is your most underused free tool — check it weekly for quick win opportunities
3. AI Overviews reward structured, factual, question-and-answer content
4. Always turn off Chrome extensions before auditing a site — they can give false readings
5. Cloudflare is free and makes your site faster and more secure — there's no reason not to use it

**Homework:** Go into Google Search Console → Performance → Queries. Filter for positions 11–20. Pick the top 3 pages. Add one FAQ section to each page targeting the exact question people are searching. Request indexing after.
